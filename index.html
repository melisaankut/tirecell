We Are TireCell

Support for Recycling

We provide support for recycling by obtaining carbon black from tires that are decomposed as waste and which are very difficult to recycle, by pyrolysis method, and using the obtained carbon black in the production of lithium-ion batteries.

Tire Waste

Around the world, approximately 14 million tons of tires are separated as waste annually. Recycling waste tires is a very difficult process. Therefore, tires that are still unusable often cause environmental pollution in landfills. Thanks to pyrolysis, which is an environmentally friendly process, we recover carbon black, which is one of the raw materials of the tire, and use it in the lithium-ion battery sector, which has seen a significant development in recent years.

PRODUCTS

Phone Battery

In the past ten years, with the rapid development of communication technology, mobile phone color screen technology, MMS technology, Bluetooth technology and camera technology have appeared successively, which puts higher requirements on the capacity, volume, weight and electrochemical performance of the battery.

Traditional liquid lithium batteries have become increasingly unable to adapt to new demands. The emergence of a new polymer lithium-ion battery (Li-Polymer) caters to this demand.

Laptop Battery

Li-Ion Battery is the type of battery you want to have in your laptop. This type is lightweight and performs better than the other types of batteries. Unlike NiCad or NiMH batteries, lithium-ion batteries don’t have the dreaded “memory effect.” The memory effect is the property of nickel-cadmium batteries that causes them to lose their capacity for full recharging if they are discharged repeatedly the same amount and then recharged without overcharge before they have fully drained.

There’s usually a rapid-charging option with lithium-ion batteries, which is good when you’re in a time crunch. Finally, this type of battery is more environmentally friendly than the other types.

Electric Car Charger

Where internal combustion engined cars get energy from burning petrol or diesel, an electric vehicle gets its power directly from a big pack of batteries. These are much like a scaled up version of the lithium-ion (Li-ion) battery in your mobile phone. This battery type don't use a single battery like a phone, they use instead a pack which is comprised of thousands of individual Li-ion cells working together.

Lithium-ion batteries have higher energy densities than lead-acid batteries or nickel-metal hydride batteries, so it is possible to make the battery size smaller than others while retaining the same storage capacity.This results in an increase in travel distance.

Power Bank

All power banks feature mainly two types of batteries: Lithium Ion (Li-ion) and Lithium Polymer (LiPo). Li-ion batteries come in a cylindrical-shaped plastic case. These batteries require low maintenance and are cheaper than LiPo batteries but have a higher energy density.

There’s usually a rapid-charging option with lithium-ion batteries, which is good when you’re in a time crunch.Considering that it will be charged repeatedly, it has become the most useful form of battery.
